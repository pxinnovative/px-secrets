# Trademark Policy

## The Basics

**"PX Secrets"** and the PX Secrets logo are trademarks of **PX Innovative Solutions Inc.**

This project is open-source under the AGPL-3.0 license, which gives you broad rights to use, modify, and distribute the **code**. However, the license does not grant rights to the **name** or **logo**.

## What You CAN Do

- Use, modify, and distribute the source code under the terms of the AGPL-3.0 license
- Reference "PX Secrets" when describing the origin of your fork (e.g., "Based on PX Secrets" or "Forked from PX Secrets")
- Use the name "PX Secrets" in factual, non-commercial contexts (reviews, articles, comparisons, academic work)
- Link to this repository

## What You CANNOT Do

- Use the name **"PX Secrets"** (or confusingly similar names like "PXSecrets", "PX-Secrets", "PX Secrets Pro") for your modified version or distribution
- Use the PX Secrets logo or PX Innovative branding in your fork or derivative work
- Imply that your modified version is endorsed by, affiliated with, or an official release from PX Innovative Solutions Inc.
- Use the name or logo in any way that suggests official status or creates confusion with the original project

## If You Fork This Project

**Rename it.** Pick a new name and logo that are clearly distinct from PX Secrets. This is the single most important thing.

You're free to mention that your project is based on or derived from PX Secrets — in fact, we encourage it. Just make the distinction clear.

Examples:
- "MyVault — based on PX Secrets" (with your own name and branding)
- "SecretKeeper, a fork of PX Secrets" (clearly identified as a fork)

## Why This Matters

Trademark protection isn't about restricting your freedom — it's about protecting the community. When someone downloads "PX Secrets," they should know they're getting the real thing: tested, maintained, and backed by the original team. Unofficial versions using the same name create confusion and erode trust.
